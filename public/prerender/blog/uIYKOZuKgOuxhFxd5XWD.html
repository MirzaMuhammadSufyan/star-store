Inkjet vs Laser Printers: Total Cost Over Years, Not Just the Box Price

That £49 inkjet looks like a steal until cartridges cost more than the printer. Here is how to compare inkjet, laser, and tank systems honestly.

<p>Printers are sold like razors: cheap hardware, expensive consumables. A showroom bargain inkjet can cost more per year than a laser that looked expensive on day one.</p> <p>Smart shopping compares <strong>cost per page</strong>, duty cycle, and how often you actually print — not just the sticker on the box.</p> <h2>Inkjet Strengths</h2> <p><strong>Photo quality</strong> — inkjets with multiple colour tanks produce smooth gradients and glossy prints unmatched by entry lasers.</p> <p><strong>Colour documents</strong> — brochures, school projects, and crafts favour inkjet.</p> <p><strong>Compact price</strong> — budget inkjets cost little upfront for occasional use.</p> <p><strong>Tank models</strong> — Epson EcoTank, Canon MegaTank, HP Smart Tank reduce per-page cost with refill bottles instead of cartridges.</p> <h2>Laser Strengths</h2> <p><strong>Text clarity</strong> — sharp black text, fast first page, no smudging on highlighters.</p> <p><strong>Volume printing</strong> — lower cost per page for office documents at scale.</p> <p><strong>Shelf life</strong> — toner does not dry out if you print monthly instead of daily.</p> <p><strong>Speed</strong> — mid-range lasers chew through stacks faster than consumer inkjets.</p> <h2>The Cartridge Trap</h2> <p>Classic cartridge inkjets price replacement sets near printer cost. &quot;XL&quot; cartridges help somewhat. Subscription ink programs trade convenience for lock-in.</p> <p>If you print rarely, dried inkjets clog nozzles — another hidden cost.</p> <h2>Cost Per Page Math</h2> <p>Estimate monthly pages. Divide cartridge or toner yield by price. Include colour if you print presentations.</p> <p>Example rough ranges (vary by model):</p> <ul> <li>Budget inkjet cartridge: high cents per page</li> <li>Laser toner: lower cents per black page at volume</li> <li>Refillable tank inkjet: very low colour and black costs after higher upfront</li> </ul> <p>Spreadsheet the five-year total before buying.</p> <h2>Home Office vs Student vs Photo Enthusiast</h2> <p><strong>Occasional forms and tickets</strong> — laser or tank inkjet; avoid cheapest cartridge models.</p> <p><strong>Students</strong> — laser for essay drafts; inkjet if design coursework demands colour proofs.</p> <p><strong>Photo printing</strong> — dedicated photo inkjet or online print service instead of forcing a laser.</p> <p><strong>Small business</strong> — duplex laser with network scanning often beats consumer inkjets on reliability.</p> <h2>Features Worth Paying For</h2> <ul> <li>Automatic duplex (double-sided) saves paper</li> <li>ADF scanners for document feeds on multifunction devices</li> <li>AirPrint / Mopria for driverless mobile printing</li> <li>Ethernet for stable office sharing</li> </ul> <p>Skip fax unless you truly still fax.</p> <h2>Wireless Printing Reliability</h2> <p>Wireless printing sounds simple until a printer drops off the home network and refuses to reconnect without a full factory reset — a common complaint across both inkjet and laser categories, though budget models tend to suffer more. Printers that sit in sleep mode to save power sometimes take noticeably longer to wake and rejoin the network, adding delay before the first page prints.</p> <p>Wired Ethernet connections avoid this instability entirely and are worth prioritising for printers used daily in a home office, even if it means running a short cable to the router. For occasional household printing, Wi-Fi convenience usually outweighs the rare reconnection hassle, but reading recent reviews for connectivity complaints on your specific shortlisted model is worth the five minutes before buying.</p> <h2>Reliability and Maintenance</h2> <p>Consumer inkjets frustrate with paper jams and head cleaning cycles. Lasers weigh more but tolerate neglect better.</p> <p>Read reviews for feed mechanism quality — cheap trays warp and misfeed.</p> <h2>Environmental Note</h2> <p>Cartridge waste accumulates. Tank systems and laser toner recycling programs reduce landfill impact. Print only what you need — digital signatures replace many paper workflows.</p> <h2>Quick Decision Guide</h2> <table> <thead> <tr> <th>You print…</th> <th>Consider</th> </tr> </thead> <tbody> <tr> <td>Rarely, black text only</td> <td>Compact laser</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Photos and colour weekly</td> <td>Tank inkjet or photo inkjet</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Hundreds of pages monthly</td> <td>Mid-range laser MFP</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Almost never</td> <td>Library or print shop — no ownership</td> </tr> </tbody> </table> <p>The cheapest printer on the shelf is rarely the cheapest printer to own. Run the ink math before checkout and you will avoid the most common home office regret.</p>
