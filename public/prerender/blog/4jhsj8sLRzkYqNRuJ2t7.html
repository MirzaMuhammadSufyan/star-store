Complete Beginner's Guide to Starting a Blog That Makes Money

Niche, domain, hosting, WordPress, SEO basics, and monetisation — a step-by-step path from zero to your first earned dollar without pretending traffic appears overnight.

<p>Starting a blog that makes money is still one of the most accessible online businesses — but the path is slower than affiliate marketers on YouTube promise. You are building a searchable asset: each helpful article can send traffic for years if you choose the right niche and stay consistent.</p> <p>This guide walks beginners from idea to monetisation without skipping the boring steps that determine success.</p> <h2>Step 1: Choose a Niche You Can Sustain</h2> <p>A profitable niche sits at the intersection of:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Search demand</strong> — people ask questions you can answer</li> <li><strong>Monetisation</strong> — affiliates, ads, or products exist</li> <li><strong>Your endurance</strong> — you can publish for twelve months without burning out</li> </ul> <p>Good examples: specific hobbies, tool comparisons, regional buying guides, career skills, problem-solving for parents or homeowners.</p> <p>Weak examples: &quot;general lifestyle,&quot; motivational quotes, news you cannot outrank major outlets on.</p> <p><strong>Exercise:</strong> Write twenty article titles before buying a domain. If you stall at five, rethink the niche.</p> <h2>Step 2: Buy a Domain and Hosting</h2> <p><strong>Domain tips:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Short, easy to spell, avoid hyphens -.com still signals trust; country TLDs fine for local topics</li> </ul> <p><strong>Hosting tips:</strong></p> <ul> <li>Shared WordPress hosting is fine to start (SiteGround, Bluehost, Cloudways, etc.)</li> <li>Prioritise support and uptime over the cheapest annual plan</li> <li>Enable free SSL (HTTPS) — non-negotiable for SEO and trust</li> </ul> <p>Budget roughly $50–150 for year one domain + basic hosting.</p> <h2>Step 3: Set Up WordPress</h2> <p>WordPress powers most independent blogs for good reason: plugins, themes, and hiring help are easy.</p> <p><strong>Launch checklist:</strong></p> <ol> <li>Install WordPress via host one-click installer</li> <li>Choose a fast, clean theme (GeneratePress, Kadence, Astra — avoid bloated multipurpose demos)</li> <li>Essential plugins: SEO (Rank Math or Yoast), caching, backups, security (limit plugins to what you use)</li> <li>Create core pages: About, Contact, Privacy Policy</li> <li>Connect Google Search Console and Analytics</li> </ol> <p>Publish a simple &quot;About&quot; story — readers and Google both prefer sites with identifiable humans behind them.</p> <h2>Step 4: Plan Content Around Search Intent</h2> <p>Money follows traffic; traffic follows relevance.</p> <p><strong>Article types that earn:</strong></p> <ul> <li><strong>How-to guides</strong> — &quot;how to fix…&quot;, &quot;how to choose…&quot;</li> <li><strong>Comparisons</strong> — &quot;X vs Y&quot;, &quot;best Z for [use case]&quot;</li> <li><strong>Problem/solution</strong> — &quot;why does…&quot;, &quot;signs you need…&quot;</li> </ul> <p>Use free keyword tools (Google autocomplete, AlsoAsked, Ubersuggest free tier) to validate topics. Target questions you can answer better than page one results — often with clearer structure, updated year, or personal testing.</p> <p><strong>Publishing cadence:</strong> One quality post weekly beats five thin posts. Aim for 1,500–2,500 words on pillar topics when depth is warranted.</p> <h2>Step 5: On-Page SEO Basics (Without Obsessing)</h2> <p>For each post:</p> <ul> <li>One primary keyword in title, URL slug, first paragraph naturally</li> <li>Descriptive H2/H3 subheadings</li> <li>Internal links to related posts</li> <li>Compressed images with alt text</li> <li>Meta description that earns the click</li> </ul> <p>Do not keyword-stuff. Write for humans; structure for crawlers.</p> <h2>Step 6: Build Trust (E-E-A-T)</h2> <p>Google rewards experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust — especially on money and health topics.</p> <ul> <li>Show author name and bio</li> <li>Cite sources for claims</li> <li>Update posts when products or facts change</li> <li>Disclose affiliate relationships clearly</li> </ul> <h2>Step 7: Monetisation Paths (In Sensible Order)</h2> <p><strong>Stage 1 — Affiliate marketing</strong></p> <p>Join programmes relevant to your niche (Amazon Associates, software affiliates, niche networks). Embed honest recommendations inside genuinely helpful content.</p> <p><strong>Stage 2 — Display ads</strong></p> <p>Apply to Mediavine, Raptive (AdThrive), or Ezoic once traffic thresholds are met — typically tens of thousands of monthly sessions. AdSense works earlier but pays less.</p> <p><strong>Stage 3 — Digital products</strong></p> <p>Sell templates, checklists, or a focused ebook to your email list.</p> <p><strong>Stage 4 — Services or consulting</strong></p> <p>Your blog becomes proof of expertise; clients find you through articles.</p> <p>Skip stage 4 monetisation fantasies until stage 1 traffic exists.</p> <h2>Step 8: Email List From Day One</h2> <p>Social algorithms change; search updates; email is yours.</p> <p>Offer a simple lead magnet — checklist, starter guide — and place signup forms in posts and sidebar. Weekly or biweekly value emails beat daily sales blasts.</p> <h2>Realistic Income Timeline</h2> <table> <thead> <tr> <th>Period</th> <th>Typical outcome</th> </tr> </thead> <tbody> <tr> <td>Months 1–3</td> <td>Little traffic, learning SEO</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Months 4–8</td> <td>First affiliate clicks, indexing gains</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Months 9–18</td> <td>Meaningful sessions if niche and consistency hold</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Year 2+</td> <td>Compound growth, raise ad tiers, diversify offers</td> </tr> </tbody> </table> <p>Many blogs earn $0 for six months. That is normal, not failure — unless you quit at month two.</p> <h2>Common Beginner Mistakes</h2> <ul> <li>Choosing a niche because it pays, not because you can publish fifty articles</li> <li>Ignoring site speed and mobile layout</li> <li>Copying AI output without editing or experience</li> <li>Monetising with spammy ads before trust exists</li> <li>No internal linking strategy</li> </ul> <h2>Your First 30 Days Action Plan</h2> <ol> <li>Finalise niche and twenty topic ideas</li> <li>Register domain, launch WordPress</li> <li>Publish About page + three cornerstone articles</li> <li>Submit sitemap to Search Console</li> <li>Share posts where your audience already gathers (forums, groups — not spam)</li> <li>Start post four through ten on a calendar</li> </ol> <p>A blog that makes money is a small business disguised as writing. Treat it with schedules, quality standards, and patience — and the income becomes a consequence of useful work, not a lottery ticket.</p>
