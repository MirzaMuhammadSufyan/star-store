Complete Beginner's Guide to Starting a Blog That Makes Money

Niche, domain, hosting, WordPress, SEO basics, and monetisation — a step-by-step path from zero to your first earned dollar without pretending traffic appears overnight.

Starting a blog that makes money is still one of the most accessible online businesses — but the path is slower than affiliate marketers on YouTube promise. You are building a searchable asset: each helpful article can send traffic for years if you choose the right niche and stay consistent.

This guide walks beginners from idea to monetisation without skipping the boring steps that determine success.

Step 1: Choose a Niche You Can Sustain

A profitable niche sits at the intersection of:

  • Search demand — people ask questions you can answer
  • Monetisation — affiliates, ads, or products exist
  • Your endurance — you can publish for twelve months without burning out
Good examples: specific hobbies, tool comparisons, regional buying guides, career skills, problem-solving for parents or homeowners.

Weak examples: "general lifestyle," motivational quotes, news you cannot outrank major outlets on.

Exercise: Write twenty article titles before buying a domain. If you stall at five, rethink the niche.

Step 2: Buy a Domain and Hosting

Domain tips:

  • Short, easy to spell, avoid hyphens
  • .com still signals trust; country TLDs fine for local topics
Hosting tips:

  • Shared WordPress hosting is fine to start (SiteGround, Bluehost, Cloudways, etc.)
  • Prioritise support and uptime over the cheapest annual plan
  • Enable free SSL (HTTPS) — non-negotiable for SEO and trust
Budget roughly $50–150 for year one domain + basic hosting.

Step 3: Set Up WordPress

WordPress powers most independent blogs for good reason: plugins, themes, and hiring help are easy.

Launch checklist:

1. Install WordPress via host one-click installer 2. Choose a fast, clean theme (GeneratePress, Kadence, Astra — avoid bloated multipurpose demos) 3. Essential plugins: SEO (Rank Math or Yoast), caching, backups, security (limit plugins to what you use) 4. Create core pages: About, Contact, Privacy Policy 5. Connect Google Search Console and Analytics

Publish a simple "About" story — readers and Google both prefer sites with identifiable humans behind them.

Step 4: Plan Content Around Search Intent

Money follows traffic; traffic follows relevance.

Article types that earn:

  • How-to guides — "how to fix…", "how to choose…"
  • Comparisons — "X vs Y", "best Z for [use case]"
  • Problem/solution — "why does…", "signs you need…"
Use free keyword tools (Google autocomplete, AlsoAsked, Ubersuggest free tier) to validate topics. Target questions you can answer better than page one results — often with clearer structure, updated year, or personal testing.

Publishing cadence: One quality post weekly beats five thin posts. Aim for 1,500–2,500 words on pillar topics when depth is warranted.

Step 5: On-Page SEO Basics (Without Obsessing)

For each post:

  • One primary keyword in title, URL slug, first paragraph naturally
  • Descriptive H2/H3 subheadings
  • Internal links to related posts
  • Compressed images with alt text
  • Meta description that earns the click
Do not keyword-stuff. Write for humans; structure for crawlers.

Step 6: Build Trust (E-E-A-T)

Google rewards experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust — especially on money and health topics.

  • Show author name and bio
  • Cite sources for claims
  • Update posts when products or facts change
  • Disclose affiliate relationships clearly

Step 7: Monetisation Paths (In Sensible Order)

Stage 1 — Affiliate marketing

Join programmes relevant to your niche (Amazon Associates, software affiliates, niche networks). Embed honest recommendations inside genuinely helpful content.

Stage 2 — Display ads

Apply to Mediavine, Raptive (AdThrive), or Ezoic once traffic thresholds are met — typically tens of thousands of monthly sessions. AdSense works earlier but pays less.

Stage 3 — Digital products

Sell templates, checklists, or a focused ebook to your email list.

Stage 4 — Services or consulting

Your blog becomes proof of expertise; clients find you through articles.

Skip stage 4 monetisation fantasies until stage 1 traffic exists.

Step 8: Email List From Day One

Social algorithms change; search updates; email is yours.

Offer a simple lead magnet — checklist, starter guide — and place signup forms in posts and sidebar. Weekly or biweekly value emails beat daily sales blasts.

Realistic Income Timeline

| Period | Typical outcome | |--------|-----------------| | Months 1–3 | Little traffic, learning SEO | | Months 4–8 | First affiliate clicks, indexing gains | | Months 9–18 | Meaningful sessions if niche and consistency hold | | Year 2+ | Compound growth, raise ad tiers, diversify offers |

Many blogs earn $0 for six months. That is normal, not failure — unless you quit at month two.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Choosing a niche because it pays, not because you can publish fifty articles
  • Ignoring site speed and mobile layout
  • Copying AI output without editing or experience
  • Monetising with spammy ads before trust exists
  • No internal linking strategy

Your First 30 Days Action Plan

1. Finalise niche and twenty topic ideas 2. Register domain, launch WordPress 3. Publish About page + three cornerstone articles 4. Submit sitemap to Search Console 5. Share posts where your audience already gathers (forums, groups — not spam) 6. Start post four through ten on a calendar

A blog that makes money is a small business disguised as writing. Treat it with schedules, quality standards, and patience — and the income becomes a consequence of useful work, not a lottery ticket.
